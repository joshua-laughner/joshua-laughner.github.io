Current research

My current research focues on using the Total Carbon Column Observing Network (TCCON) to validate the Orbiting Carbon Observatory 3 (OCO-3) when it launches early in 2019. OCO-3 will be placed on the International Space Station, and due to the orbit of the ISS, will pass overhead at different local times each day. This contrasts with OCO-2, which always passes over a given location at roughly the same time each day. This means that the combination of OCO-2 and OCO-3 will let us study how certain processes change over the course of a day.

The main product of OCO-3 will be its XCO2 measurement, the mixing ratio of CO2 in the total atmospheric column. An important step in using OCO-3 is to verify that this XCO2 measurement is accurate. Like OCO-2, we will use the TCCON network to do so. TCCON is a network of high spectral resolution FT-IR spectrometers that measure column XCO2 from the ground. Because these can be calibrated very precisely, they provide the key method to validate OCO-3 by comparing coincident measurements.
