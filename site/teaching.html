What is the role of the professor in the information age?

With information literally at students' finger tips, the professor's role must become the curator and guide to information, rather than the source

I grew up in the 1990s and early 2000s, attending elementary school and middle school as the internet transformed from a new innovation to a principal source of information. I was halfway through high school before Wikipedia had one million articles. I remember using Microsoft Encarta on CDs in elementary school and AskJeeves in middle and high school.

This experience has shaped my views on teaching. Now, students rely on teachers and professors as repositories of information less than ever, simply because so much information is available online. But because so much information is available online, students need a guide to help them navigate it all. Therefore, in addition to being an expert in my fields that can transmit my own understanding to my students, I see my resposibilities to be:

  1. A curator of knowledge. As an expert in my fields, I can sort through the available information and direct students to resources I determine are accurate and provide unique perspectives on the information. The more perspectives and methods presentation I can bring to my students, the better I can help those with different styles of learning than myself.
  2. A guide through the information. A student first entering a new subject can't know which topics to cover first, but I, as an expert, know how the topics within that subject fit together, and so can direct the student through them in an sensible order.
  3. Finally, as an instructor, to set tasks for the students (or help them design the tasks themselves) that give them experience using the information they obtain from myself or any source, and in the process, internalize and synthesize that information.
These roles are not new; these are roles that teachers have carried out for centuries, if not millenia. Only the sources and availability of information changed. And while the ability of the internet to connect students with instructors around the world is truly new, I believe it is still crucial for students to have one instructor they can meet face-to-face, and whose credentials they know and can trust.
