Teaching experience

At the University of California, Berkeley:
  • Graduate student instructor for Chem 15 (analytical chemistry), Fall 2015
    • Instructed a 25 student lab section on topics including electrochemistry and electrochemical titration, spectrophotometry, GC/MS, and column chromatography
    • Held semiweekly office hours and lab and lecture material
  • Graduate student instructor for Chem 4A (general chemistry for majors), Fall 2013 and Fall 2014
    • Instructed a 20-24 student lab section on topics including titration, absorbance and fluorescence spectroscopy, and computational chemistry
    • Led one course-wide discussion section each semester, reviewing lecture topics, e.g. particle in a box and the Schrödinger equation
    • Held semiweekly office hours on lab and lecture material
  • Mentoring younger graduate students and undergraduate students in research:
    • I worked extensively with a new graduate student for over a year, teaching her about remote sensing of NO2 and numerical chemical models for her work on constraining lightning NOx emissions with space-based measurements.
    • I worked with an undergraduate student for approximately one year, mentoring her in a project using remote sensing of NO2 to study the effects of peroxy acetyl nitrate on transport of NOx out of urban areas. This work resulted in paper currently in preparation.
  • I also spent significant time teaching several group memebers about various topics, including:
    • Scientific programming
    • Numerical modeling
    • Version control
    • Using supercomputer clusters, and
    • Using the WRF-Chem model
At the Pennsylvania State University:
  • Instrument room TA, Fall 2011
    • Instructed student use of GC, FTIR, and NMR; guided students in data interpretation
  • Chemistry tutor (general and organic), 2010-2013
    • Instructed individuals and small groups, primarily through working problems, as well as reviewing lecture notes.
